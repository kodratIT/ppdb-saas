## Customer Insights

### Customer Behavior Patterns

Private educational institutions in Indonesia are increasingly adopting SaaS solutions to automate administrative burdens. Post-pandemic, there is a shift from "optional" to "essential" digital infrastructure. Schools seek "professionalism" as a differentiator against competitors. Families now expect a "seamless experience" where pendaftaran can be done entirely via mobile, reflecting the nationwide high smartphone penetration.
_Source: https://www.medcom.id, https://digitalsociety.id_

### Pain Points and Challenges

The "Negeri-minded" philosophy is the biggest external challenge; private schools must compete with "perceived free" government schools. Internally, manual data verification often leads to "data fatigue" and errors. Furthermore, digital literacy among parents in remote or tier-2/3 cities can lead to abandonments if the UI is too complex.
_Source: https://www.kompasiana.id, https://jahe.or.id_

### Decision-Making Processes

Decisions for EdTech adoption are typically top-down, driven by the Foundation (Yayasan) Board or the Principal. Key criteria include:

1. **Automation of Selection:** Needs to be objective to avoid "titipan" (referral) controversies.
2. **Integrated Finance:** Must handle VA/E-wallet payments for registration fees.
3. **Local Support:** Accessibility of the vendor via WhatsApp/Technical support is a deal-breaker.
   _Source: https://sevenmediatech.co.id, https://skoolacloud.id_

### Customer Journey Mapping

The journey starts with **Awareness** (Social media ads/Word of mouth), follows by **Registration** (Mobile-friendly web form), **Verification** (Real-time WhatsApp notification), **Payment** (Automated VA check), and ends with **Result Announcement** (Digital card printing). Friction in the "Payment" and "Verification" steps often leads to high drop-off rates.
_Source: https://cerdig.com, https://drabsky.com_

### Customer Satisfaction Drivers

The primary drivers of satisfaction for school admins are **Time-saving** (automated ranking) and **Data Accuracy**. For parents, it is **Transparency** and **Ease of Use**. Integration with WhatsApp for status updates is cited as one of the most appreciated features in the Indonesian market.
_Source: https://ppdbsekolah.com, https://jicnusantara.com_

### Demographic Profiles

- **School Admins/Principals:** Age 35-55, varying tech-literacy, focus on efficiency and enrollment conversion.
- **Parents:** Age 25-45, high mobile usage, value speed and clear communication.
  _Source: https://pgrijawatengah.com_

### Psychographic Profiles

- **The Modern Foundation:** Values prestige and technological leadership.
- **The Efficiency-Driven Admin:** Values reducing clerical errors and manual paperwork.
- **The Apprehensive Parent:** Values security of data and simple, guided processes.
  _Source: Based on market behavior analysis_

## Competitive Landscape

### Key Market Players

1. **SIAP PPDB (Telkom SIAP Online):** The undisputed leader in the public sector. Cloud-based, highly integrated with government procurement (SPBE).
2. **Jibas (Jaringan Informasi Bersama Antar Sekolah):** Strong in private foundations. Open-source core with paid support/modules.
3. **PPDBSekolah.com:** Pure SaaS focused on speed and mobile-accessibility.
4. **E-ujian.id:** Aggressive "free-to-premium" model, serving 2000+ schools with integrated CBT and SPP modules.
5. **MySCH.id (PT Edu Tekno Indonesia):** Focuses on branding and school profile integration.
   _Source: https://siap-ppdb.com, https://jibas.net, https://e-ujian.id_

### Market Share Analysis

While exact percentages are private, usage data shows **SIAP PPDB** serves over 5,454 schools as of 2024, with a heavy concentration in DKI Jakarta. The private school market is a "Red Ocean" of small-to-medium localized providers. EdTech market in Indonesia as a whole is estimated at **$3.23 Billion** (2024), with SaaS being the fastest-growing sub-sector.
_Source: https://bps.go.id, https://imarcgroup.com_

### Competitive Positioning

- **SIAP PPDB:** Positions as "Official & Compliant" (The Government Choice).
- **Jibas:** Positions as "Complete & Customizable" (The Foundation Choice).
- **PPDBSekolah.com:** Positions as "Modern & Simple" (The Cost-Effective Choice).
- **PPDB-SAAS-V2 (The Project):** Opportunity to position as **"Performance & Marketing Oriented"** (Focusing on high-concurrency edge architecture and conversion).
  _Source: Competitive feature audit_

### Strengths and Weaknesses

- **Strengths (Competitors):** High trust (SIAP), Complete SIS modules (Jibas), Massive existing user base (E-ujian).
- **Weaknesses (Competitors):** Often slow/clunky UI on mobile, high complexity for setup, lack of advanced applicant-tracking/marketing metrics.
  _Source: User feedback surveys on EdTech forums_

### Market Differentiation

The major differentiation opportunity lies in **Edge-Side Performance** (Cloudflare Workers) and **Zero-Infrastructure Setup**. While competitors like Jibas might require some server knowledge, this project offers an "Instant-on" multi-tenant experience with superior mobile speed.
_Source: Analysis of TDD v1.0 vs Competitor Stacks_

### Competitive Threats

- **Government Monopolies:** If governments extend SIAP PPDB to all swasta schools for free.
- **Feature Creep:** Competitors building payment gateways (like Jibas SchoolPay) faster.
- **Giant EdTechs:** Large players like Ruangguru potentially entering the admission SaaS space.
  _Source: https://east.vc_

### Opportunities

- **Regulasi SPMB 2025:** New government efforts to include private schools in official admission cycles create a massive need for integrated, reliable SaaS bridges.
- **Cloudflare/Neon Stack:** Leveraging modern serverless to offer lower subscription costs while maintaining higher performance than legacy SQL/VM based competitors.
  _Source: https://rri.co.id, TechInAsia_

## Research Summary and Conclusions

### Executive Summary

The Indonesian PPDB SaaS market is at a turning point with the upcoming 2025 SPMB regulations. While public schools are dominated by traditional players like Telkom, the private sector remains fragmented, tech-hungry, and underserved in terms of performance and marketing integration. The shift towards mobile-first consumption among parents makes high-performance edge computing (the Cloudflare/Neon stack) a major competitive advantage.

### Key Conclusions

1. **Product-Market Fit:** Confirmed. There is a high willingness to pay among private school foundations for systems that improve professionalism and branding.
2. **Competitive Edge:** Speed and Simplicity. Existing systems are often perceived as "clunky" or "complex" to set up.
3. **Primary Risk:** Infrastructure complexity for the development team, which can be mitigated by sticking to established serverless patterns.

### Strategic Recommendations

- Focus on the **Private School Foundation (Yayasan)** segment as the primary entry point.
- Mirror the **SIAP PPDB** compliance features while beating them on **UX and Speed**.
- Use **WhatsApp Integration** as a primary marketing tool for schools to nurture leads.
